## 1. How to Convert Word (DOCX) to PDF Locally
Formatting issues are the enemy of professional documents. Converting Word to PDF ensures that your fonts, margins, and layouts remain identical on any device.

### Steps to Convert:
1.  Visit the [Word to PDF Converter](/tools/document/word_to_pdf).
2.  Drag your `.docx` or `.doc` file into the active zone.
3.  BladeTools will automatically render the document into a high-quality PDF.
4.  Click **Download PDF** to save the result.

**Best For:** Resumes, academic papers, and business proposals where layout precision is non-negotiable.

## 2. How to Merge and Combine Multiple PDF Files
Managing separate files for a single project can be chaotic. Combining them into one organized PDF makes distribution much easier.

### Steps to Combine:

## 3. How to Split Large PDFs or Extract Specific Pages
Sometimes, you receive a 100-page document but only need three specific pages to send to a client.

### Steps to Split:
1.  Open the [PDF Splitter & Page Extractor](/tools/document/pdf_split).
2.  Upload the master PDF.
3.  **Visual Selection:** Click the thumbnails of the pages you want to keep, or enter a custom range (e.g., `1, 5, 12-15`).
4.  Click **Split and Download**. You will receive a new PDF containing only the pages you chose.

## 4. How to Protect Your Brand with PDF Watermarks
Watermarking is a crucial step for protecting intellectual property and preventing unauthorized distribution. BladeTools offers two distinct ways to do this.

### Method A: Adding Text Watermarks
1.  Navigate to [Add Text Watermark to PDF](/tools/document/pdf_add_text_watermark).
2.  Type your text, such as "INTERNAL USE ONLY" or "CONFIDENTIAL."
3.  Customize the font size, opacity, and rotation to ensure it's visible but doesn't block the content.

### Method B: Adding Image or Logo Watermarks
1.  Go to the [Add Image Watermark to PDF](/tools/document/pdf_add_image_watermark) tool.
2.  Upload your company logo (preferably a transparent PNG).
3.  Position the logo on the page. You can place it in a corner like a stamp or center it as a background overlay.
4.  Process the file to "burn" the image into the PDF structure.

## 5. How to Edit PDF Metadata for Professionalism
The "Properties" of your PDF (the Title, Author, and Subject) are often overlooked. However, they appear in search engine results and file explorers.

### Steps to Update Metadata:
1.  Use the [PDF Metadata Editor](/tools/document/pdf_metadata_updater).
2.  Upload your file and view the existing tags.
3.  Update the **Title** to match your document name, add your name as the **Author**, and include relevant **Keywords**.
4.  Click **Update**. This is a vital step for SEO if you plan on hosting the PDF on your website.
